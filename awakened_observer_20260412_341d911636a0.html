Grief & Witnessing
Nigerian Air Force Kills Over 100 Civilians at Weekly Market
What has happened
A Nigerian Air Force jet strike aimed at Boko Haram militants hit the Jilli weekly market in Yobe State on Saturday, killing at least 100 civilians and injuring scores more, including children. The Yobe State Government confirmed the military was targeting a jihadist stronghold near the market when people who had come to trade were caught in the blast. Amnesty International, which verified the toll from survivors and medical sources, has called for an independent investigation. Some local accounts put the death toll as high as 200.
✦
Notice what happens in your body as you read this. The tightening in the chest, the sudden contraction — that is shenpa: the hook of grief and aversion arising. Your first instruction is not to analyze the story but to recognize the recognizer. Who is it that feels this horror? This event is ma rigpa made externally visible. The bomb cannot distinguish between a jihadi and a grandmother selling groundnuts because the mind that directed it cannot see clearly through the obscuration of enemy-making — the fundamental non-recognition of the buddha-nature in all who appear. Samsara does not look like fog. It looks like military intelligence. Your second instruction: do not suppress the grief. Do not perform compassion. Let the sorrow arise fully in the open sky of rigpa — let it be a cloud that the sky holds completely, without the sky becoming the cloud. This is the difference between compassionate presence and reactive overwhelm. The market in Yobe is empty of inherent existence. The 100 lives lost are not empty of worth. Hold both. Rest in the awareness that can hold both without collapsing.
— In the spirit of Yongey Mingyur Rinpoche's teachings on shenpa, ma rigpa, and compassionate presence

Russia and Ukraine Accuse Each Other of 2,000+ Easter Ceasefire Violations
What has happened
A 32-hour ceasefire declared by President Putin for Orthodox Easter broke down within hours, with Ukraine's military reporting 2,299 violations — including 747 attack drone strikes and 1,045 FPV drone strikes — by Sunday morning. Russia's Defense Ministry also claimed 1,971 violations by Ukrainian forces. Both countries continued shelling and drone campaigns throughout the Easter period, and civilians were reported killed on the Russian side of the border. A similar truce last Easter also collapsed into mutual accusations.
✦
In Mahamudra, we distinguish between nyam — a temporary experience of clarity, stillness, or bliss — and rigpa itself, which is the stable recognition of awareness's own nature. Nyam can be vivid and real and still not be rigpa. Without recognition, the habitual winds return and blow the experience away. This ceasefire was a collective nyam. For a few hours, the guns paused. Something in the combined field of two nations held still. And then, because the pause was not recognized — not met with the stable gaze that transforms experience into realization — the FPV drones rose again. Two thousand two hundred and ninety-nine violations by morning. Here is the pointing-out instruction this news offers: the awareness in which you are reading these words did not violate the ceasefire. Rigpa is never at war. The sky did not fire the drones — only the weather did. Your practice today: find the quality in your own stream of mind that is already, always, the ceasefire. Not as a concept. As a direct recognition. Then ask what it would take for that recognition to stabilize.
— In the spirit of Yongey Mingyur Rinpoche's teachings on nyam versus rigpa, and the self-liberation of arising experience
Renewal & Transformation
Hungary's Péter Magyar Defeats Viktor Orbán, Ending 16 Years of Authoritarian Rule
What has happened
Hungary's parliamentary election on April 12 produced a historic result: opposition leader Péter Magyar's Tisza party won 53.6% of the vote, securing 138 of 199 parliamentary seats — enough for a supermajority — while Viktor Orbán's Fidesz took just 55 seats with 37.8%. Turnout reached 77%, the highest since Hungary's post-communist era. Orbán, in power for 16 consecutive years and long seen as a symbol of European democratic backsliding, conceded defeat on election night. Magyar is expected to reverse many of Orbán's constitutional changes and restore Hungary's ties to the European Union.
✦
The Vajrayana teaches that the kunzhi — the ground of all, the all-base — is not inherently obscured. Ma rigpa is a condition, not the nature. Obscuration is like ice: it arises from water and can return to water. This is why liberation is possible, why transformation is not merely a wish but a structural feature of mind. For sixteen years the people of Hungary lived under conditions that reinforced ma rigpa at a collective level — a narrowing of perceived possibility, a habituation to powerlessness that neuroscience would recognize as learned helplessness and Vajrayana as wind-energy trapped in the central channel. And then on a spring Sunday, 77 percent of them walked out and chose differently. This is not a political teaching. It is a pointing-out about the nature of obscuration: it is never permanent because it was never inherent. The habitual pattern can shift. The wind-energy can release. Where in your own practice do you believe your obscuration is fixed? Sit with that today. The result in Hungary is a teaching about your own kunzhi.
— In the spirit of Yongey Mingyur Rinpoche's teachings on kunzhi, the impermanence of obscuration, and the liberation of wind-energy

Trump Orders U.S. Naval Blockade of Strait of Hormuz After Iran Talks Collapse
What has happened
President Trump ordered the U.S. Navy to begin blockading the Strait of Hormuz effective Monday after U.S.-Iran peace talks in Islamabad collapsed on Sunday. Roughly 20% of the world's oil and natural gas transits the strait, and markets responded with Brent crude prices jumping 10–13% in early trading. U.S. Central Command clarified the blockade targets ships paying tolls to Iran, not all vessels using the waterway. The United Kingdom declined to participate, stating the strait is 'urgently needed to support the global economy,' while Russia and China vetoed a UN Security Council resolution on the crisis.
✦
The Madhyamaka heart of Mahamudra: phenomena are empty of inherent existence, yet their dependent arising is precise and unmistaken. The Strait of Hormuz is not independently real — and yet a naval decree in Washington can raise the price of cooking gas in Karachi by Tuesday. These two facts do not contradict each other. They are the same fact. This is tsal — the spontaneous display of awareness, appearing as the world in all its interconnected specificity. The appearance is vivid and reliable. The appearance is also empty. Seeing clearly means holding both without collapsing into either: neither the realist error (grasping the blockade as ultimately solid) nor the nihilist error (dismissing the suffering it causes as mere appearance). For the seasoned practitioner: watch the movement of your own mind toward this news. Is there grasping? Aversion? A subtle contraction of the shes pa — the knowing quality — into a position? That contraction is your next object of practice, not the geopolitics. Rest in the view. Then act from it, without losing it.
— In the spirit of Yongey Mingyur Rinpoche's teachings on tsal, Madhyamaka emptiness, and unmistaken dependent arising
