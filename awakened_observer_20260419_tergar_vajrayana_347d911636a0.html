I was bathed with acid meant for my brother – Anambra graduate
What has happened
Obanye Francess, a Biochemistry graduate from Anambra, Nigeria, became an acid attack survivor at age nine when she was mistakenly targeted instead of her brother. The attack, stemming from a family conflict, left her with severe burns. Despite immense physical pain, emotional trauma, and societal discrimination, Francess completed her education. She now operates an online dropshipping business, diversifying her income to overcome employment challenges faced by burn survivors. She also recently experienced the loss of both her parents.

When we hear about the immense suffering endured by Obanye Francess, it naturally stirs our compassion. To face such pain, both physical and emotional, at a young age, and yet to persevere, to complete her education – this is truly remarkable. It shows us something profound about the human spirit, about our inherent capacity for resilience. This is not to diminish the pain, but to acknowledge the potential for awakening that can emerge even from the most tragic circumstances. We witness her outer wounds, but perhaps her inner journey has revealed an indestructible wisdom.

Experimental drug doubles one-year survival in pancreatic cancer
What has happened
A new experimental drug has demonstrated the ability to double the one-year survival rate for individuals with advanced pancreatic cancer in a recent trial. This drug functions by enhancing the susceptibility of tumors to both chemotherapy and the body's immune system. This development is considered a notable advancement in the treatment of pancreatic cancer, which is known for its aggressive nature and poor prognosis.

The new status underscores the urgent need for conservation efforts to protect these iconic inhabitants of the polar regions from further decline.

Israel-Lebanon ceasefire begins as Iran keeps Strait of Hormuz gridlocked
What has happened
A 10-day ceasefire has been initiated between Israel and Hezbollah in Lebanon, providing a temporary cessation of hostilities. This truce aims to create a window for negotiations to resolve the broader conflict. Simultaneously, the Strait of Hormuz is experiencing significant disruption to shipping, as Iran reversed its decision to reopen the waterway and engaged vessels attempting passage, citing the continued US blockade of Iranian ports. These concurrent developments illustrate both fragile peace efforts and persistent geopolitical challenges in the region.
