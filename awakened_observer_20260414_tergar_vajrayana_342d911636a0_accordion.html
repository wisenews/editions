7 Days of Intensive Meditation Can Rewire Brain and Body, UC San Diego Study Finds
What has happened
A recent study by UC San Diego researchers, published in Communications Biology, demonstrates that just seven days of intensive meditation and mind-body practices can induce significant biological changes. Participants exhibited improved brain efficiency, enhanced immune system signaling, and increased levels of the body's natural pain-relieving compounds. The findings suggest that mental practices can rapidly influence physical health by activating systems involved in brain flexibility, metabolism, and immune function, with some effects mirroring psychedelic-like brain states.

Wisdom teaching
✦
This UC San Diego study, showing how quickly our brains and bodies respond to meditation, is truly wonderful. It might seem like science is "rewiring" us, but from a contemplative view, it's more like uncovering what's already there. Our natural state is one of balance and clarity, and these practices simply help us remove the obscurations that prevent us from experiencing it. When the mind settles, the body follows, and vice versa. It’s not about becoming something new, but recognizing our intrinsic wholeness. The "improved efficiency" isn't a new feature, but the system returning to its optimal, unburdened function. This deep connection reminds us that true well-being isn't something external we acquire, but an inner landscape we reveal.
— In the spirit of Mingyur Rinpoche's teaching on The Intrinsic Wholeness of Mind and Body

Practice prescription
Wonder & Awe Practice Transcending
Find a comfortable seated posture, allowing your spine to be naturally upright. Close your eyes gently or keep them softly gazing downwards. For the next five minutes, bring your attention to the sensations of your body: the feeling of your breath moving in and out, the contact of your body with the cushion or chair, the warmth or coolness of the air on your skin. Allow your awareness to rest openly with these sensations, without judgment or trying to change anything. Just be present with the body's natural state. When thoughts arise, acknowledge them gently, then return to the body. Conclude by offering a simple wish for well-being to yourself and all beings.

From the Mahamudra perspective, the scientific findings of brain and body "rewiring" through meditation are simply the display of rigpa (primordial awareness). There is no "rewiring" needed for that which is already primordially pure and complete. The perceived changes are tsal (the dynamic manifestation) of the mind's inherent clarity and spaciousness, not an alteration of its fundamental nature. The body and mind, inseparable, are recognized as the dharmakaya (truth body) itself, manifesting its qualities when the obscurations of shes pa (conceptual mind) loosen their grip. It is a recognition, a rang grol (self-liberation), not a fabrication.

Lebanese and Israeli Officials Hold First Direct Diplomatic Talks in Decades Amid Regional Tensions
What has happened
Lebanese and Israeli officials engaged in their first direct diplomatic talks in Washington, D.C., since 1993 on Tuesday. These discussions, which included Secretary of State Marco Rubio, aim to resolve the ongoing conflict between Israel and Hezbollah. The talks are unfolding against a backdrop of continued regional hostilities, including a U.S. blockade of Iranian ports and Pakistan's efforts to facilitate a second round of U.S.-Iran peace negotiations.

Sit comfortably, with an upright and relaxed posture. Bring to mind the leaders and people of Lebanon and Israel, as well as all those affected by conflict everywhere.
