Grief & Witnessing
Nigerian Air Force Kills Over 100 Civilians at Weekly Market
What has happened
A Nigerian Air Force jet strike aimed at Boko Haram militants hit the Jilli weekly market in Yobe State on Saturday, killing at least 100 civilians and injuring scores more, including children. The Yobe State Government confirmed the military was targeting a jihadist stronghold near the market when people who had come to trade were caught in the blast. Amnesty International, which verified the toll from survivors and medical sources, has called for an independent investigation. Some local accounts put the death toll as high as 200.
✦
Take a breath before you read this. Let the air move fully into your body, and know that in a village in Yobe State, there are families who will not breathe easily today. A market is a sacred gathering. It is where people bring what they have grown, what they have made — to nourish each other. The mother who came with her children to sell groundnuts did not know the sky would become dangerous. The farmer who came for seed did not know he would not return. The military said there was intelligence. The market said there were people. Between intelligence and people, the bomb could not tell the difference. Interbeing means: what kills there diminishes here. The grief of the Jilli market is not a foreign grief. It belongs to all of us who live in systems that make war, and to all of us who know that each human life is irreplaceable. Do not turn the page quickly. Let the weight of this land in you. That landing — that felt acknowledgment — is itself a form of solidarity.
— In the spirit of Thich Nhat Hanh's teaching on interbeing and the sanctity of ordinary life

Russia and Ukraine Accuse Each Other of 2,000+ Easter Ceasefire Violations
What has happened
A 32-hour ceasefire declared by President Putin for Orthodox Easter broke down within hours, with Ukraine's military reporting 2,299 violations — including 747 attack drone strikes and 1,045 FPV drone strikes — by Sunday morning. Russia's Defense Ministry also claimed 1,971 violations by Ukrainian forces. Both countries continued shelling and drone campaigns throughout the Easter period, and civilians were reported killed on the Russian side of the border. A similar truce last Easter also collapsed into mutual accusations.
✦
There is a reed cut from the reed bed who cannot stop crying. Listen: this is not a story of hatred. It is a story of longing so catastrophic it no longer knows how to put down the weapon. Putin declared a ceasefire for Easter — the holiest of pauses, the feast of the stone rolled away — and within the hour the drones were already flying. Two thousand, two hundred and ninety-nine violations by morning. Each one a moment when a human being chose the trigger over the wound they could not yet admit was there. Rumi would say: the wound is the place where the Light enters. But first you must be willing to stop opening new wounds long enough to feel the old ones. Easter is the day the stone is supposed to roll away. This Easter, both armies pushed it back in place and called it military necessity. The longing for peace lives in every soldier. The tragedy is that the machinery of war is louder than longing. Be with the longing today — yours and theirs both. It is the only part still free.
— In the spirit of Rumi's teaching on longing and the wound, from the Masnavi
Renewal & Transformation
Hungary's Péter Magyar Defeats Viktor Orbán, Ending 16 Years of Authoritarian Rule
What has happened
Hungary's parliamentary election on April 12 produced a historic result: opposition leader Péter Magyar's Tisza party won 53.6% of the vote, securing 138 of 199 parliamentary seats — enough for a supermajority — while Viktor Orbán's Fidesz took just 55 seats with 37.8%. Turnout reached 77%, the highest since Hungary's post-communist era. Orbán, in power for 16 consecutive years and long seen as a symbol of European democratic backsliding, conceded defeat on election night. Magyar is expected to reverse many of Orbán's constitutional changes and restore Hungary's ties to the European Union.
✦
We all want to be free. This seems obvious — and yet, politically, it is still radical. In Hungary on Sunday, 77 percent of people went out to vote. The highest turnout since communism ended. These were people who, for sixteen years, had been told in many different ways that their voice did not matter, that the strong man knew best, that questioning power was disloyalty. And yet on Sunday morning, they walked to the polling station. The great teaching of interdependence — pratītyasamutpāda — tells us that no condition lasts forever. Everything that arises depends on causes and conditions, and when those conditions shift, the situation shifts with them. Sixteen years of accumulated cause and effect — and then, on a spring Sunday, something turned. I would ask us to hold this moment with care: not as a triumph over enemies, but as the restoration of a people's basic dignity. That is worth celebrating quietly, with a full heart. And I would also ask: what in your own life feels permanent today that is, in truth, only a condition waiting for its causes to change?
— In the spirit of His Holiness the Dalai Lama's teaching on impermanence and collective dignity
Power & Collective Karma
Trump Orders U.S. Naval Blockade of Strait of Hormuz After Iran Talks Collapse
What has happened
President Trump ordered the U.S. Navy to begin blockading the Strait of Hormuz effective Monday after U.S.-Iran peace talks in Islamabad collapsed on Sunday. Roughly 20% of the world's oil and natural gas transits the strait, and markets responded with Brent crude prices jumping 10–13% in early trading. U.S. Central Command clarified the blockade targets ships paying tolls to Iran, not all vessels using the waterway. The United Kingdom declined to participate, stating the strait is 'urgently needed to support the global economy,' while Russia and China vetoed a UN Security Council resolution on the crisis.
✦
A decree is issued in Washington. Oil prices jump in London. Markets shudder in Tokyo. A family in Karachi finds the price of cooking gas has doubled by Tuesday. This is not metaphor. This is how the world is built: interlocked, interdependent, immediate. The Stoics called it sympatheia — the mutual tensioning of all things. Marcus Aurelius wrote from his military tent that the welfare of rational beings is bound together. He was right in ways he could not have fully imagined. Here is what is within your control: your understanding of how these systems work, and how your own choices — what you consume, who you support, what you permit in your name — participate in chains of consequence that cross oceans. Here is what is not within your control: the Navy, the negotiations, the price of crude. Distinguish carefully between these two columns. Then act on the first with full attention, and release your grip on the second. This is not resignation. It is the only form of power that cannot be blockaded, sanctioned, or seized.
— In the spirit of Marcus Aurelius / Epictetus on sympatheia and the discipline of assent
